Game #1: Boomerang Warrior

This is the second to last game concept I tried to develop very seriously. I particularly focused in on the graphics and special effects, and the games overall polish. It's probably the most complete Scratch project I've ever made, even though it literally doesn't have an ending... Or a way to lose... Or to win...

In this game, you play as the green lizard in the center of the screen, and the aim of the game is to try and stop the robot army from reaching you using your trusty boomerang. You aim the boomerang by holding down the left-mouse button (which also shows the predicted flight path of the boomerang) and then release it to throw the boomerang and that chosen path.
While the boomerang is flying through the air, you can press the left mouse button again to make it fly at super speeds, giving you more control over the timing of it's flight path.

Game #2: Untitled Race car Game

This one's a little more involved, and a lot less finished. I never even incorporated a system for spawning enemies endlessly so it's basically unplayable, just a demo of some unfinished ideas.

This game actually has three control sets (get out your pen and paper):

  • The arrow keys, which controls your car...
  • "WASD" and "IJKL", which controls the shape of the level...
  • And the mouse, which creates enemies when clicking on the level, and coins when clicking off the level.

Pressing up on the arrow keys makes the car jump, and the left and right keys make you drive in either direction. You can jump onto enemy's heads like Mario, which will make you bounce off them high into the air, each bounce launching you higher than before.
The amount of damage you deal to enemy's is based on how high you bounced, so the longer you can bounce without missing an enemy will finish enemy's faster.
While in mid air, you can also press the space bar to drop bombs, which was the first power up I planned to implement. You have a limited number of bombs to drop, but killing enemy's gets you more.
Coins don't do anything.

I planned on having many different types of enemies and multiple levels, which would include destructible elements so they would evolve as you played.
